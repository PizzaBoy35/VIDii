1. Eeveelution guide

    Since 1996, Pokémon has taken over the world and spawned an incredicle amount of games, shows, merchandise and more. Catching new Pokémon, fighting/trading them with others and going on an adventure together is the exact appeal of this giant franchise, all starting at the original Pokémon Red and Blue games for the Nintendo Gameboy. One of the most popular Pokémon to explain the ways of evolution is Eevee, the 2nd mascot after Pikachu.

    But Eevee's evolutions can be quite complicated when compared to the rest. That is because Eevee has 8 potential different evolutions, all with different types and stats. That is what this interface is all about. Start by navigating to the right or use the shortcut menu in the upper left corner to find out about all so-called Eeveelutions and the ways that you can get them in the games.

  3. Eeveelution guide

    Eevee evolves into Jolteon by giving it a thunderstone.

    Jolteon, the Lightning Pokémon. Jolteon’s cells generate a low level of electricity. This power is amplified by the static electricity of its fur, enabling the Pokémon to drop thunderbolts. The bristling fur is made of electrically charged needles.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/jolteon

  4. Eeveelution guide

    Eevee evolves into Vaporeon by giving it a waterstone.

    Vaporeon, the Bubble Jet Pokémon. Vaporeon underwent a spontaneous mutation and grew fins and gills that allow it to live underwater. This Pokémon has the ability to freely control water. Its cell composition is similar to water molecules. As a result, it can melt away into water.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/vaporeon

    Flareon, the Flame Pokémon. Flames burn within a saclike organ inside this Pokémon. When Flareon inhales, these flames grow in intensity, reaching a mighty 3,000 degrees Fahrenheit.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/flareon

  6. Eeveelution guide

    Eevee evolves into Espeon by having max happiness and leveling up during the day. Happiness can be achieved by battling with Eevee, giving it vitamins or poffins, and simply walking with the Pokémon.

    Espeon, the Sun Pokémon. Espeon is extremely loyal to any trainer it considers to be worthy. It is said that this Pokémon developed its precognitive powers to protect its trainer from harm. It unleashes psychic power from the orb on its forehead. When its power is exhausted, the orb grows dull and dark.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/espeon

  7. Eeveelution guide

    Eevee evolves into Umbreon by having max happiness and leveling up during the night. Happiness can be achieved by battling with Eevee, giving it vitamins or poffins, and simply walking with the Pokémon.

    Umbreon, the Moonlight Pokémon. When exposed to the moon’s aura, the rings on its body glow faintly and it gains a mysterious power. It hides silently in darkness and waits for its foes to make a move. When this Pokémon becomes angry, its pores secrete a poisonous sweat, which it sprays at its opponent’s eyes.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/umbreon

  8. Eeveelution guide

    Eevee evolves into Leafeon by leveling it up near a mossy rock. The mossy rock can be found in multiple regions. For example: Eterna Forest in Sinnoh, Pinwheel Forest in Unova and Lush Jungle in Alola.

    Leafeon, the Verdant Pokémon. Its cellular composition is closer to that of a plant than an animal. It uses photosynthesis to produce its energy supply without eating food. This Pokémon’s tail is blade sharp, with a fantastic cutting edge that can slice right though large trees, but it rarely fights. Galarians favor the distinctive aroma that drifts from this Pokémon’s leaves.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/leafeon

  9. Eeveelution guide

    Eevee evolves into Glaceon by leveling it up near an icy rock. The Icy Rock can be found in multiple regions. For example: Frost Cavern in Kalos, Mount Lanakila in Alola and Shoal Cave in Hoenn.

    Glaceon, the Fresh Snow Pokémon. It can control its body temperature at will. This enables it to freeze the moisture in the atmosphere, creating flurries of diamond dust. The coldness emanating from Glaceon causes powdery snow to form, making it quite a popular Pokémon at ski resorts.

    More info about this Pokémon: https://www.pokemon.com/nl/pokedex/glaceon
